The underinflated tire will produce a round bump at the bottom of the wheel (As shown in image 1), while a inflated tirewill not (As shown in image 2).

  • Find and locate the recomended tire PSI.

Find the Max tire pressure or PSI, located around the rim of the tires (Seen in pictures 5, 6, 7, 8).

A good rule of thumb is to have the tire's PSI 80% of its max.

  • Check your tires PSI

Find your tires valve stem (located in the rim of the wheel).

Check your tires current PSI -- make sure its at least 80% of its max PSI -- using a pressure gauge on the valve stem.

Add Air To Your Deflated Tire

  • Add Air!

Start pumping air.

Make sure to check the tires PSI periodically, to insure that it doesn't get overinflated.

Once you reach the desired air pressure --80% of its max PSI-- then insure that the rest of the wheel have the same amount of PSI.
